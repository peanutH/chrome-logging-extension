    The Process of Leaf Color Change

    The process of leaf color change in autumn is a complex interplay of chemistry and biology. As the season progresses, the amount of chlorophyll in leaves decreases, revealing the hidden pigments that contribute to the autumn colors. The transformation is triggered by the decline in daylight hours and the onset of cooler temperatures, which signal the trees to prepare for winter. The process involves the breakdown of chlorophyll, the presence of carotenoids, and the synthesis of anthocyanins, leading to the vibrant hues we see in autumn leaves.
