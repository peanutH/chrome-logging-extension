    Coffee originated in the Ethiopian highlands in the 9th century, where locals first chewed energizing coffee cherries. It spread to the Arab world in the 15th century, becoming a cultural staple via Sufi monks in Yemen. Today, it fuels billions as the world’s second most traded commodity. 
    1. Ethiopian Origins (circa 9th Century) 
    • The Legend of Kaldi: According to lore, an Ethiopian goat herder named Kaldi discovered the energizing effects of the plant after his goats ate the bright red berries and became unusually energetic.
    • Initial Consumption: Early Ethiopian tribes and Sufi monks in the Harar region crushed the berries, mixed them with animal fat, or steeped them as a medicinal tea to maintain focus during long hours of prayer.
